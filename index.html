Working hypothesis What breaks first when a mortgage infrastructure company scales through this inflection.

Here is what breaks first when a mortgage infrastructure company grows through that inflection. The AI pre-approval layer that handles clean income, clean assets, and clean liabilities keeps performing. The edge cases do not. Self-employed borrowers, non-QM, complex-income structures, jumbo. Every one of those cases routes to a human underwriter, and every one of them arrives with partial context. The reviewer re-gathers what the model already saw. The compliance audit trail fragments. The loan slows. The secondary-market pricing on that loan reflects the delay.

This is a routing problem, and routing problems are orchestration problems. We read most companies against a 60/30/10 frame.

60%
Traditional code

Database work, state management, the boring 60% of any origination stack.

30%
Rule-based logic

Disclosure, state licensing, product eligibility, TRID windows. The compliance surface.

10%
Genuine AI

Document extraction, narrative underwriting on complex cases, anomaly flagging.

Most mortgage-tech builds get those proportions backwards. The AI gets the attention. The 30% rule layer is where the compliance exposure actually lives, and the 60% code layer is where the operational scale actually lives.

Precedent · Regulated document workflows

We mapped the same shape at VigilOre, a multi-agent compliance platform for regulated document workflows.

Specialist agents for each compliance domain, rule-based routing between them, human review on the edge cases, every decision logged in an interpretable audit trail. For a mortgage-infrastructure company entering its scaling year, that architecture is directly portable. TRID windows replace DRC licensing rules. Non-QM underwriter escalation replaces geological-data review. The orchestration pattern is the same.
